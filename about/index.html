AboutAustin, TX

Structure makescomplexity usable.

UX designer, educator, and builder. I make complex information clear, then I ship it.

I taught music before I designed for the web, and the two disciplines rest on the same idea. Music is a structural system that helps people understand and interpret sound much like information architecture does for content.

For three years at idfive in Baltimore, I untangled complex information for universities and nonprofits, including Howard University, Johns Hopkins, the Smithsonian, and the University of Maryland. I led discovery research, built information architecture, and managed a design system of more than 300 components. I earned idfive's Rising Star award in 2023 and contributed to a Webby-winning project in 2024.

I hold a Master of Professional Studies in User Experience Design from the Maryland Institute College of Art and a Bachelor of Education from the University of Texas Pan-American. Both degrees stay busy: I teach computer science and cybersecurity at Leander ISD, and I design and build for small businesses and nonprofits across central Texas. Research, architecture, accessible design, and the front-end code to ship it. Band of the Hills is what that looks like end to end.

If your information is complex and your users are lost, that is my favorite kind of problem.
