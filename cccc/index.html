Central CarolinaCommunity College

A community college with a vast catalog, restructured so every audience can find its way in.

Clientcccc.edu ↗
RoleUX Design
Agencyidfive
The client

Central Carolina Community College serves a wide region of North Carolina with a catalog that runs from associate degrees and diplomas to certificates, job training, and high school programs. Its audiences range from first-time students to university transfers, adult learners, veterans, employers, and alumni.

The problem

A vast catalog with no clear way in.

Students could not navigate the certificate, diploma, and degree offerings. The homepage opened no clear paths into the sections of the site where that information lived, the many audiences the college serves had no routes of their own, and athletics was an afterthought.

The question

How might we open clear paths through a vast catalog, give every audience a route of its own, and bring athletics into focus?

Move 01 / 04

The homepage

I rebuilt the homepage around the two questions students actually arrive with: what career do I want, and what can I do here? Career Communities group programs by career goal, and a plain task list opens the college's main paths, from earning an associate degree to finishing high school, getting job training, or reading it all in Spanish. Each one is a direct route into the architecture rather than a menu to decode.

Move 02 / 04

The audiences

The college serves many distinct audiences and the old site addressed them all at once. I gave each one a dedicated path in the Info For menu, from current high school students and adult learners to military and veterans, employers, and alumni. Visitors identify themselves in a single click and land in content written for them.

Move 03 / 04

The program finder

Certificates, diplomas, and degrees were hard to tell apart and harder to compare. I built a finder that searches programs by career and routes to a career community page where every credential is laid out together: what you will learn, length, credits to graduate, locations, and the curriculum guide. Interest in programs rose once it launched.

Move 04 / 04

Athletics

Athletics had no real home on the site. I gave it a highlighted space and built roster and schedule templates that every team shares, so each sport publishes consistently without custom work. The teams became a visible part of the college's story instead of a footnote.

05
Impact

The redesign turned a large, hard to navigate catalog into a set of clear routes. Students can search by career or by goal, each audience has a path written for them, and athletics has a visible home. Interest in programs rose after the finder launched, the teams returned to focus, and the restructured architecture tells the college's story.
