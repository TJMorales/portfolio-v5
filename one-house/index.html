One HouseAt A Time

A home-buying system that lets clients move through the entire bidding process independently, online.

Clientonehousebaltimore.org ↗
RoleUX Design
Agencyidfive
The client

One House At A Time is a government-adjacent nonprofit revitalizing Baltimore neighborhoods by restoring vacant properties. Clients bid on properties, renovate, and sell, creating a system for rebuilding the city.

The problem

The process lived on paper, and nobody could see the whole path.

The site could not carry clients through the process. Initial steps, important dates, and follow-through were unclear, and applications lived on paper, so few visitors became completers of the end-to-end flow.

The question

How might we create a digital application experience that reduces friction and allows clients to complete the entire process independently?

Move 01 / 03

Properties

Audiences struggled to find property details and called the office to ask staff about locations. I built faceted search with new property detail pages: a one-page snapshot of every location at auction, past and present.

Move 02 / 03

Processes

The bidding process confused people from start to finish. I rebuilt the architecture around process and success, added a section for important dates, and put the One House steps on the homepage, so clients always know what comes next and when.

Move 03 / 03

Personalization

Stakeholders and clients wanted a curated experience. I created a login flow with a fully online bidding application, a zip-code selector for properties in chosen areas, and a dashboard with alerts for upcoming dates, auctions, and events.

03
Impact

The redesign gave One House a platform clients can use on their own. Property information lives in one searchable place, the bidding process is legible from start to finish, and the application that used to travel by mail is completed online.
